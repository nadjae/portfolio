WRKOUT

The WRKOUT web page is the result of prototyping throughout different levels of fidelity. The goal was to create a technical tool for someone deeply involved and engaged in their sport on a daily basis. The final prototype became both a tool for the WRKOUT members to communicate and schedule new WRKOUTS and a marketing platform to attract new participants.

Context

9 week cours in prototyping at the Department of Computer and System Siences, DSV, Stockholm University.

Goal

The goal was to practice prototyping, from low to hig fidelity, and to create a suiting UI.

Core values

Throughout interviews with WRKOUTs founder some core values for the desired tool was identified:

• target audience 25 - 50 years old

• encouraging

• motivating

Persona

WRKOUTs members got to fill out a form and based on the responses a persona was created, to get a better picture of how the target audience interact with technology and what their motivation and pain points were.

The created persona.

Defined needs

Two main request were identified during the research phase; the WRKOUT members needed both an easier way to coordinat workouts but also a place where all their information could be gathered as a way to attract potential future participants.

Low fidelity prototypes

The first rough sketches and wireframes did not fullfill all the requirements identified since they did not allow the WRKOUT members to schedule their workouts, but it did enable user testing targeting the partial functions and interface that potential participant would be facing when approaching the website.

First sketches.

Wireframes.

Elaborating the design

The graphic design language of WKROUT is meant to mediate what a modern way of excercising that WRKOUT actually is. Especially the font family of the logo and the color palett reflects this ambition. In combination with modernity, it felt important to convey the forgiving and easy-going atmosphere that WRKOUT offers. With that in mind a more relaxed font family was chosen for the remaining text and a playfull additional logo was designed.

The graphic was done in Illustrator and the interactions in Adobe XD.
