WRKOUT

The WRKOUT web page is the result of prototyping throughout different levels of fidelity. The goal was to create a technical tool for someone deeply involved and engaged in their sport on a daily basis. The final prototype contained both a tool for the WRKOUT members to communicate and schedule new WRKOUTS, and a marketing platform to attract new participants.

Context

9 week course in prototyping at the Department of Computer and System Sciences, DSV, Stockholm University.

Note: since the project carried out in the course was group work, I have here recreated a project following the same work procedure as in the course.

Goal

The goal was to practice prototyping, from low to high fidelity, and to create a suiting UI.

Core values

Throughout interviews with WRKOUTs founder some core values for the desired tool was identified:

• target audience 25 - 50 years old

• encouraging

• motivating

Persona

WRKOUTs members got to fill out a form and based on the responses a persona was created, to get a better picture of how the target audience interacts with technology and what their motivation and pain points were.

The created persona.

Defined needs

Two main requests were identified during the research phase; the WRKOUT members needed both an easier way to coordinate workouts but also a place where all their information could be gathered as a way to attract potential future participants.

Low fidelity prototypes

The first rough sketches and wireframes did not fulfill all the requirements identified but they did allow the WRKOUT members to schedule their workouts, a function that turned out not as intuitive as thought. It would require a lot of rework and later on a second round of user testing since many users got stuck when trying to find the right booking features in the initial prototypes.

First sketches.

Early wireframes.

Elaborating the design

The graphic design language of WRKOUT is meant to mediate what a modern way of exercising that WRKOUT actually is. Especially the font family of the logo and the color palette reflects this ambition. In combination with modernity, it felt important to convey the forgiving and easy-going atmosphere that WRKOUT offers. With that in mind a more relaxed font family was chosen for the remaining text and a playful additional logo was designed.

High fidelity prototypes

The graphic was done in Illustrator and the interactions in Adobe XD. The final user tests were conducted on the prototypes presented below, indicating that some minor changes could benefit the solution if put into development. The main problem identified consisted of the "contact us" page being placed below the login page that is intended only for members, one could therefore argue that the login page could have been placed as the last feature in the menu.
