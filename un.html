Participatory design and The Global Goals

This participatory design project aimed to result in a system supporting two of the UN’s global goals for sustainable development and involved different sorts of brainstorming, contextual inquiry, prototyping together with participants representative for the user group, validation throughout different methods, followed by a number of iterations of prototyping.

Lessons Learnt:

To let users prototype is to allow users to explore, express and test hypotheses about future ways of living. This is, in my opinion, a perfect way to let different user perspectives lead the way through a design process. Prototyping generally constitutes a tool that enables exploration of the entire design space including it's possible alternatives.

Context

15 ECTS course in Participatory Design at the Department of Computer and System Sciences, DSV, Stockholm University.

Goal

The goal was to develop a system related to the UN’s global goals through participatory design.

Contextual inquiry

Throughout contextual inquiry, carried out remotely using zoom, some core topics for the system were identified. We as a project group interviewed and observed the participants engaged in their everyday activities for a couple of hours. This was done with our chosen UN goals as starting point and focus, together with our target group that we after an initial 635-brainstorm agreed on: young adults. The core topics identified, related to our two UN goals, concerned the reasons as to why we make environmental friendly decisions:

• availability

• information

• price

• shame

• amusement

Future workshop

Our participants got to elaborate even further on our selected goals through a future workshop, but this time our core topics identified in the previous phase constituted the delimitation of the session. The future workshop was summarized in hindsight by the project members and resulted in a mind map.

Affinity diagrams

With guidance from the project team, affinity diagrams of different sorts were arranged by the participants throughout the three phases of the future workshop.

An affinity diagram from the initial, fantasy, phase of the future workshop.

Users prototyping

To let users themselves prototype is used as a method to create representations of future potential services and to visualize what these could concretely mean. In this project, three methods were used; scenarios, storyboards and sketching. A scenario is a written story that describes how a user uses the system to solve a certain task. A storyboard, or a comic strip, describes functionality and how the user uses a system in relation to a timeline. Finally, when sketching a user sketches the system that he/she visualizes, including different elements reaching from functionality to visual elements.
